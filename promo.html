Wagering Requirements Explained

The wagering requirement (also called playthrough) is the most important condition of any bonus. It is a multiplier that determines how many times you must bet your bonus funds or winnings from free spins before they can be converted into real, withdrawable cash.

Maximum Cashout Limits

Maximum cashout limits are common, especially with no-deposit offers. This is a cap on the amount you can withdraw from winnings earned with a bonus.

Game Contributions and Restrictions

Not all games contribute equally toward meeting wagering requirements. The bonus terms specify the contribution percentage for each game type. A typical structure is:

  • Slot Games: 100% contribution – every C$1 bet counts as C$1 toward wagering.
  • Table Games (Blackjack, Roulette): 5–10% contribution – every C$1 bet counts as only C$0.05–C$0.10.
  • Live Casino Games & Certain Jackpot Slots: 0% contribution – bets do not count toward wagering.

To maximize efficiency, focus on playing eligible slot games that contribute 100% toward the wagering requirements.

Time Limits and Expiry Dates

Bonuses don't last forever. They come with strict time limits. You will have a certain number of days to claim an offer, a certain number of days to use the bonus spins/credits, and a certain number of days to complete the wagering requirements. Failure to meet these deadlines will result in the forfeiture of the bonus and any associated winnings.
